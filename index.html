Projects

LDPC Codes Implementation for Video Transmission

For my final year project during my engineering degree at the University of Pretoria, I undertook the challenging task of implementing LDPC (Low-Density Parity-Check) codes in hardware. Leveraging the power of STM32 embedded platforms, I successfully developed a robust system that demonstrated exceptional performance in video transmission applications. The system boasted impressive achievements, including a Bit Error Rate (BER) below 10^-4 within 2 dB of the Shannon limit. This outstanding result was achieved with a code rate of 0.5, a codeword length of 4096 bits, and an impressive decoding rate of 4096 bits/s. These accomplishments not only showcased my dedication to pushing the boundaries of technology but also highlighted my ability to optimize system parameters for optimal performance. This project was not only a culmination of my academic journey but also a testament to my commitment to excellence. It played a pivotal role in my graduation with distinction, underscoring my proficiency in both theoretical concepts and hands-on implementation. The LDPC codes implementation for video transmission is a proud milestone in my career, reflecting my passion for cutting-edge technology and my capability to deliver tangible and high-impact solutions to real-world challenges. Join me in celebrating the intersection of hardware, signal processing, and video communication in this exciting journey of technological exploration.
